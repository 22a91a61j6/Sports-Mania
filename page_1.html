Cricket is one of many games in the "club ball" sphere that involve hitting a ball with a hand-held implement.A key difference is the existence of a solid target structure, the wicket (originally, it is thought, a "wicket gate" through which sheep were herded), that the batter must defend.The cricket historian Harry Altham identified three "groups" of "club ball" games: the "hockey group", in which the ball is driven to and from between two targets (the goals); the "golf group", in which the ball is driven towards an undefended target (the hole); and the "cricket group", in which "the ball is aimed at a mark (the wicket) and driven away from it".
  • Players and Teams
  • Teams: Each team has 11 players.
  • Substitutes: Allowed for fielding injuries but cannot bowl, bat, or act as captain.
  • Captain: Leads the team and makes strategic decisions.
  • Game Structure
  • Innings: Each team gets one or two innings depending on the match format.
  • Overs: Each over consists of 6 legal deliveries (balls).
  • Formats:
  • Test Cricket: Played over five days with two innings per team.
  • One Day International (ODI): 50 overs per side.
  • Twenty20 (T20): 20 overs per side.
  • Scoring
  • Runs: Scored by batsmen running between wickets or hitting the ball to the boundary.
  • Boundary: 4 runs if the ball hits the ground before crossing the boundary line; 6 runs if it crosses the boundary without touching the ground.
  • Extras: Additional runs awarded for no-balls, wides, byes, and leg byes.
  • Dismissals
  • Bowled: Ball hits the stumps and dislodges the bails.
  • Caught: Ball is caught by a fielder, wicketkeeper, or bowler without touching the ground.
  • Leg Before Wicket (LBW): Ball hits the batsmanâ€™s leg in line with the stumps and would have hit the stumps.
  • Run Out: Fielder hits the stumps with the ball while batsmen are running between wickets.
  • Stumped: Wicketkeeper removes the bails while the batsman is out of his crease.
  • Hit Wicket: Batsman dislodges the bails with his body or bat while playing a shot.
  • Fielding Restrictions
  • Powerplay: Limited overs formats have fielding restrictions during the initial overs.
  • ODI: First 10 overs, maximum of 2 fielders outside the 30-yard circle.
  • T20: First 6 overs, maximum of 2 fielders outside the 30-yard circle.
  • Fielders: Maximum of 5 fielders allowed on the leg side at any time.
  • Bowling Regulations
  • No-ball: Overstepping the crease, delivering dangerous deliveries, or bowling more than the allowed number of short-pitched deliveries.
  • Wide Ball: Ball delivered too wide for the batsman to reach.
  • Over Rate: Teams must bowl their overs within a specified time.
  • Equipment
  • Ball: Made of leather, with a circumference of 22.4-22.9 cm and a weight of 155.9-163 g.
  • Bat: Must be made of wood, with a length not exceeding 38 inches and width not exceeding 4.25 inches.
  • Protective Gear: Includes helmets, pads, gloves, and abdominal guards.
