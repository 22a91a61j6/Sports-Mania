Hockey is a fast-paced sport played on grass, artificial turf, or ice, with variations such as field hockey and ice hockey. In field hockey, two teams of eleven players use curved sticks to hit a small, hard ball into the opposing team's goal. Ice hockey is played on an ice rink with six players per team, including a goaltender, using sticks to maneuver a puck into the opponent's net. Both versions of the game require skill, strategy, and teamwork, making hockey an exciting and competitive sport.
  • Team Composition:
  • Each team consists of 11 players in field hockey or 6 players in ice hockey.
  • Players include forwards, midfielders/defensemen, and a goalkeeper.
  • Game Duration:
  • Field hockey matches are typically played in two halves of 35 minutes each.
  • Ice hockey games consist of three periods of 20 minutes each.
  • Scoring:
  • A goal is scored when the ball (field hockey) or puck (ice hockey) crosses the goal line between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar.
  • The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins.
  • Offside Rule:
  • In field hockey, there is no offside rule.
  • In ice hockey, players must not enter the attacking zone ahead of the puck.
  • Stick Usage:
  • Players can only use the flat side of the stick to hit the ball in field hockey.
  • In ice hockey, players use the stick to handle the puck, but high-sticking is penalized.
  • Penalties:
  • Fouls are penalized with free hits, penalty corners, or penalty strokes in field hockey.
  • Infractions in ice hockey lead to penalties, where players serve time in the penalty box.
  • Substitutions:
  • Substitutions are allowed at any time in both field and ice hockey, but must be made from the designated substitution area.
  • Protective Gear:
  • Players must wear appropriate protective gear, including shin guards, mouth guards, and helmets in field hockey.
  • Ice hockey players wear helmets, gloves, pads, and skates for protection.
  • Face-offs and Free Hits:
  • Ice hockey games start with a face-off, and subsequent face-offs occur after stoppages.
  • Field hockey starts with a center pass, and free hits are awarded for minor infractions.
  • Goalkeeping:
  • Goalkeepers wear additional protective gear and can use any part of their body to stop the ball/puck.
  • In ice hockey, goalies have a wider stick blade and specialized equipment to block shots.
