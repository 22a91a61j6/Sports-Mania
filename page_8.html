Table tennis, also known as ping-pong, is a fast-paced sport played on a rectangular table divided by a net. Players use small paddles to hit a lightweight ball back and forth across the table, aiming to score points by making the ball land on their opponent's side of the table. The game requires quick reflexes, precise control, and strategic shot placement. It can be played in singles (one player on each side) or doubles (two players on each side). The objective is to win points by making the ball bounce twice on the opponent's side or by causing the opponent to miss the ball.
  • Game Objective: Score points by making the ball bounce twice on the opponent's side or by causing the opponent to miss the ball.
  • Serving:
    • The server must toss the ball vertically upwards at least 16 cm.
    • The ball must first bounce on the server’s side, then on the opponent's side.
    • The server must strike the ball behind the end line and above the level of the playing surface.
  • Scoring:
    • A match is typically played best of 5 or 7 games.
    • Games are played to 11 points, but a player must win by at least 2 points.
    • Players switch serves every 2 points and switch sides every game and in the deciding game when one player reaches 5 points.
  • Rally:
    • The ball must be returned before it bounces twice on a player’s side.
    • Players must hit the ball with their paddle or body before it crosses the net.
    • The ball must be struck behind the end line and above the level of the playing surface.
  • Let Serve:
    • If the serve hits the net but still goes over and lands correctly on the opponent's side, it is called a let serve and is replayed.
  • Scoring Points:
    • A point is scored if the opponent fails to make a correct service or return, hits the ball out of bounds, or allows the ball to bounce twice on their side.
  • Faults:
    • A fault is called if the server fails to toss the ball correctly, strikes the ball before it has been tossed, or does not strike the ball behind the end line.
  • Equipment:
    • Each player uses a paddle and a small, lightweight ball.
    • The table is 2.74 meters long, 1.525 meters wide, and 76 cm high, divided by a net that is 15.25 cm high.
