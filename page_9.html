Badminton is a fast-paced racquet sport played either indoors or outdoors, where players use a shuttlecock to score points against their opponents. The game is typically played by two players (singles) or two pairs (doubles). The objective is to hit the shuttlecock over a net and into the opponent's side of the court in such a way that they cannot return it successfully. Badminton requires agility, precision, and strategy, with players engaging in quick exchanges of shots that demand both skillful maneuvering and powerful strikes.
  • Objective: Hit the shuttlecock over the net and into the opponent's side of the court to score points.
  • Court: The court is rectangular and divided by a net. Dimensions vary slightly for singles and doubles play.
  • Serving:
    • Serve must be hit below the waist and diagonally across the court.
    • The server must remain within the serving area until the shuttlecock is hit.
  • Scoring:
    • Matches are usually played best of three games.
    • Each game is played to 21 points; players must win by at least 2 points.
    • Points are scored on every serve, regardless of which side served.
  • Rally:
    • The shuttlecock must be hit over the net and land within the opponent’s court.
    • Players are allowed to hit the shuttlecock once before it crosses the net.
    • The shuttlecock must not touch the ground or any other surface outside the court boundaries.
  • Faults:
    • A fault occurs if the shuttlecock lands outside the court, fails to clear the net, or if a player hits the shuttlecock before it crosses the net.
    • Players must not touch the net or cross over to the opponent’s side of the court.
  • Lets: A let is called when play is stopped due to an unforeseen or accidental occurrence, such as the shuttlecock hitting the top of the net on a serve but still going over.
